I serve as the <strong>Assistant Chair for Graduate Programs and Research</strong> and the <strong>Graduate Program Director (GPD) for the PhD Program</strong> in the <a href="https://www.odu.edu/computer-science">Department of Computer Science</a>. See our website for information on our graduate programs (<a href="https://www.odu.edu/computer-science/academics/graduate/masters">MS</a>, <a href="https://www.odu.edu/computer-science/academics/graduate/phd">PhD</a>). If you need to contact a CS GPD, please use <a href="mailto:csgpd@odu.edu">csgpd@odu.edu</a>.</p><p>For an overview of my research over the past few years, see <a href="https://items.ssrc.org/parameters/on-the-importance-of-web-archiving/">On the importance of web archiving</a>, an article I wrote for <a href="https://items.ssrc.org/category/parameters/"><em>SSRC Parameters</em></a> in 2018. I recently gave an <a href="https://circulatingnow.nlm.nih.gov/2022/11/10/whats-in-a-web-archive-collection-summarization-and-discovery-of-archived-webpages/">interview covering my background, our research group, and challenges facing web archive collections</a> for the National Library of Medicine’s <a href="https://circulatingnow.nlm.nih.gov/"><em>Circulating Now</em> blog</a>. I’m currently featured on the <a href="https://www.odu.edu/facultydevelopment/women-in-stem#tab9=3&amp;done1612907281342">ODU Faculty Women in STEM page</a>, where you can read a bit more about my background and experiences as a woman in computer science. For more information on my education and work experience, see my <a href="#bio">bio</a>.</p><p><strong>Research Interests:</strong> web science, social media, web archiving, information visualization (see some student infovis projects in my <a href="https://www.cs.odu.edu/~mweigle/research/gallery.html">infovis gallery</a>)</p><h2 id="teaching-spring-2024">Teaching (Spring 2024)</h2><p><a href="/teaching/2024-spr-cs725825">CS 725/825 - Information Visualization &amp; Data Analytics</a> / MW 3-4:15pm, ECSB 2120</p><h2 id="research">Research</h2><p>I’m a member of the ODU Web Science and Digital Libraries (WS-DL) Research Group.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://oduwsdl.github.io/" target="_blank" class="btn btn--mcw"><i class="fas fa-fw fa-link"></i><span> WS-DL Webpage</span></a> <a href="https://twitter.com/WebSciDL" target="_blank" class="btn btn--mcw"><i class="fab fa-twitter" style="color: #55acee"></i><span> WS-DL Twitter</span></a> <a href="https://ws-dl.blogspot.com/" target="_blank" class="btn btn--mcw"><i class="fab fa-blogger" style="color: #F37100"></i><span> WS-DL Blog</span></a> <a href="https://github.com/oduwsdl" target="_blank" class="btn btn--mcw"><i class="fab fa-fw fa-github" style="color: #171516"></i><span> WS-DL GitHub</span></a></p><div class="list__item"><article class="archive__item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork"><h2 class="archive__item-title" itemprop="headline"> <a href="https://weiglemc.github.io/publications/recent" rel="permalink">Recent Publications </a></h2><p class="archive__item-excerpt" itemprop="description"><ol><li>Rachel Zheng and Michele C. Weigle is a Professor of Computer Science at Old Dominion University. Her research interests include web science, social media, web archiving, and information visualization. She has published over 115 articles in peer-reviewed conferences and journals and has served as PI or Co-PI on external research grants totaling $6M from a wide range of funders, including the National Science Foundation, the National Endowment for the Humanities, the Institute of Museum and Library Services, and the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation. She currently serves on the editorial boards of the <em>Journal of the Association for Information Science and Technology</em> (JASIST) and the <em>International Journal on Digital Libraries</em> (IJDL). Dr. Weigle received her PhD in computer science from the University of North Carolina in 2003.</p><h3 id="academic-timeline">Academic Timeline</h3><ul><li>July 2018 - present, Professor, <a href="https://www.cs.odu.edu/">Computer Science</a>, <a href="https://www.odu.edu/">Old Dominion University</a></li><li>July 2012 - July 2018, Associate Professor, Computer Science, Old Dominion University</li><li>July 2006 - July 2012, Assistant Professor, Computer Science, Old Dominion University</li><li>July 2004 - July 2006, Assistant Professor, <a href="http://www.clemson.edu/ces/departments/computing/">Computer Science</a>, <a href="http://www.clemson.edu">Clemson University</a></li><li>August 2003 - June 2004, Visiting Assistant Professor, Computer Science, University of North Carolina</li><li>August 2003, Ph.D., <a href="https://www.cs.unc.edu">Computer Science</a>, <a href="https://www.unc.edu">University of North Carolina</a></li><li>May 1998, M.S., Computer Science, University of North Carolina</li><li>May 1996, B.S., <a href="http://www.ulm.edu/cba/computerscience/index.html">Computer Science</a> (<a href="http://www.ulm.edu/honors">Honors Program</a>), Northeast Louisiana University (now <a href="http://www.ulm.edu">University of Louisiana at Monroe</a>)</li></ul><iframe width="300" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/FVIg42xHlvQ" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer;
