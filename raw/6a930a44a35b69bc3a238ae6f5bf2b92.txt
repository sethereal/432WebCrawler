    function debounce(func, wait, immediate) {
        var timeout;
        return function() {
          var context = this, args = arguments;
          var later = function() {
            timeout = null;
            if (!immediate) func.apply(context, args);
          };
          var callNow = immediate && !timeout;
          clearTimeout(timeout);
          timeout = setTimeout(later, wait);
          if (callNow) func.apply(context, args);
        };
      };

            function isOnScreen(elem) {//console.log("is onscreen inside");
                // if the element doesn't exist, abort

                    return;
                }
                var $window = jQuery(window);
                var viewport_top = $window.scrollTop();
                var viewport_height = $window.height();
                var viewport_bottom = viewport_top + viewport_height;
                var $elem = jQuery(elem);
                var top = $elem.offset().top;
                var height = $elem.height();
                var bottom = top + height;

                return (top >= viewport_top && top < viewport_bottom) ||
                (bottom > viewport_top && bottom <= viewport_bottom) ||
                (height > viewport_height && top <= viewport_top && bottom >= viewport_bottom);
            }

<h3 class=" hamptonBlueHead   ">In-state Tuition Benefit for Active Duty Military</h3>

    <p><em>Effective October 1, 2016</em>, the Code of Virginia section 23.1-500--510 provides the following benefit:</p>
<p>Active duty military, activated guard or reservist members, or guard or reservist members mobilized or on temporary active orders for six months or more, <strong>who are residing in Virginia</strong>, can be eligible for the in-state tuition rate.</p>
<p>To be eligible, the active duty military member must apply for the in-state tuition rate using the <a href="https://forms.odu.edu/view.php?id=467634">Application for Active Duty/Veteran Tuition Rate</a>. Verification of eligibility is through orders and proof of Virginia residence, which should be submitted with the application.</p>
<ul><li>The benefit is <strong>not</strong> based on designation of Virginia in-state residency, but rather it places a limit on the amount of tuition the student may be charged.</li>
<li>Tuition Assistance authorization forms will not qualify students for the in-state tuition rate. The documentation specified below must be submitted in order to qualify for in-state rates.</li>
<li>The provision does not provide access to financial aid reserved for Virginia in-state residents and eligible students continue to be categorized as out-of-state (if appropriate) for reporting purposes.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Documentation required:</strong></p>
<ul><li>A copy of the military orders which verify active duty military status or activation of reservist/guard member</li>
<li>Documentation verifying Virginia residence (such as utility bill, valid lease, etc.)</li>
<li><em>You must submit this documentation EACH SEMESTER in which you plan to use this benefit</em></li>
</ul>
<p></p>

				<section class="section parsys layout-5 bg-stripes-default">


<h3 class="opensans-bold-italic hamptonBlueHead   ">*Tuition Assistance Requirements for Active Duty Military Eligibility for In-State Tuition*</h3>




    <p></p>
<p>Effective Fall 2007, tuition assistance authorization forms no longer automatically qualify students for the in-state tuition rate. To be eligible for the in-state tuition rate, active duty military members must apply for the in-state tuition rate using the Active Duty Military Tuition Benefit Form.</p>
<p>The following two elements form the basis of the in-state tuition eligibility for non-Virginia domiciled members of the armed forces enrolled in courses at Old Dominion University:</p>
<ol><li>Members of the armed forces not domiciled in Virginia must submit a completed<strong> tuition assistance (TA) document </strong>that covers at least a portion of the tuition for the semester for which they are enrolling.</li>
<li>In addition, the military student's assigned command must have a <strong>&quot;Special Arrangement Contract&quot; (SAC) </strong>with Old Dominion University.</li>
</ol>
<p><em>A TA document must be submitted for at least a portion of the student's total tuition for <strong>every semester</strong> that the military student is enrolled at ODU. The student is responsible for paying the portion of the tuition not covered by the TA, and submission of the TA document signifies that the student accepts liability for payment of the tuition not covered. This is an absolute requirement of Virginia law that governs domicile and tuition rates at state institutions and is a provision of the SAC with their command or facility. The University processes the TA document to pay the covered amount-- please always include a social security number (SSN) or University Identification Number (UIN) on TA forms.</em></p>

    <p>As defined by 37 U.S.C. 401, all dependents of:</p>
<ul><li>active duty military personnel, or activated or temporarily mobilized (activated for service for six months or more) reservists or guard members,</li>

<p><em>&quot;Dependent&quot;</em> is defined to include:</p>
<ul><li>Spouse</li>
<li>Child under the age of 21, provisions up to age 23</li>
<li>Dependent parent</li>
<li>Legal custody, adoption and step-child</li>

<p>For dependents of active duty members, the workplace can be either within Virginia, a state contiguous to Virginia, or the District of Columbia.<em> Place of residence MUST be in Virginia.</em></p>

</li>

<li>Copy of military dependent identification card issued by the U.S. military.</li>

<h4 class=" include-heading-bar skyblue hamptonBlueHead   ">VERY IMPORTANT: </br>TA Authorization Forms require a SSN or UIN</h4>




    <p><em>It is imperative to include a SSN or UIN on all tuition assistance documents</em>. Per military policy, SSN are not printed on military service member's tuition assistance (TA) authorizations. There will be blanks to supply either a Social Security number (SSN) or University ID number (UIN). If you do not provide a SSN or UIN on the form, it cannot be processed, which will cause additional billing notices and could result in <a href="https://www.odu.edu/tuition-aid/tuition/penalties">late fees, account holds, and collection fees</a>.</p>

    <p><a href="https://www.odu.edu/tuition-aid/tuition/billing/payments">Student Payments</a></p>

    <p><strong>Master of Engineering Management (MEM Ashore Program)</strong> participants are subject to all financial policies at Old Dominion University unless exceptions are specifically noted. Students are encouraged to refer to <a href="https://www.odu.edu/tuition-aid/tuition/billing">Student Billing &amp; Payments</a> and the University Catalog for detailed information on policies and procedures.</p>
<p>The MEM Ashore Program's registration is not limited to the typical semester schedule, and tuition deadlines may not coincide with enrollment periods. Accordingly, the<strong> </strong><em>tuition is due at the time of registration</em>.</p>
<p></p>
 



				<section class="section parsys"><footer class="footer parsys section">



    <p><a href="https://online.odu.edu/programs/engineering-management-by-portable-media">MEM Ashore Program</a></p>

				<div class="tab-content section">



    <p>The University does not have control over the disbursement of funds from the Veterans' Administration. Students must pay all charges by the end date of the deferment period even if have not been received. The purpose of the deferment is to permit additional time for veterans, however the time is limited and cannot be extended beyond the published deadline.</p>
<p>Students participating in educational programs through the Department of Veterans Affairs may qualify for a deferment of tuition and fees (only). Interested students should contact the <a href="https://www.odu.edu/registrar">Office of the Registrar.</a> Deferments are only granted if the appropriate paperwork has been processed by the Registrar's Office prior to the tuition deadline for each semester.</p>
<p>Veterans deferments are independent and should not be confused with other University payment arrangements. Students with debts to the University are not eligible.<br /></p>
 



				


<h3 class="    ">For Students Using VA Educational Benefits:</h3>

				



    <p>For students using VA Educational Benefits and students who are utilizing their VA Benefits may qualify for a veteran's deferment at least 90 days from the tuition deadline. Charges not covered by the VA must be paid by the <a href="https://www.odu.edu/tuition-aid/tuition/billing">tuition deadline</a> to prevent <a href="https://www.odu.edu/tuition-aid/tuition/penalties">late penalties</a>.</p>

<h3 class=" hamptonBlueHead   ">Institution Policy for Return of Unearned Tuition Assistance (TA) Funds to the Government</h3>

<p>In instances when a student stops attending due to a demonstrated military service obligation, the University will work with them to identify solutions that will not result in student debt for the returned portion.</p>
